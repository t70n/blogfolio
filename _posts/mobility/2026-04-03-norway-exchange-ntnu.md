Here is a short introduction to the different courses I am about to take at NTNU. There, my plan is to study cybersecurity, and more specifically wireless network security and data privacy, which is a subject I am getting more and more into, and finally an introduction to artificial intelligence to build a strong base before going deeper into the topic by myself, especially in network science and related models.

If you would like more information about them, feel free to click the links and visit the official university course pages.

[Wireless Network Security](https://www.ntnu.edu/studies/courses/TTM4138/2025/1#tab=omEmnet)

> In this course, we study various wireless networks and their security properties, with a focus on cryptographic protocols. The technologies covered include Bluetooth, Wi-Fi, and mobile networks. The course addresses topics that are more advanced than in introductory courses on information security and communication technology, and helps students develop their own ability to analyze and discuss the security of a system.
>
> After taking this course, we will be able to place these technologies in the context of the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), for example target 9.1 (develop quality, reliable, sustainable, and resilient infrastructure), target 9.5 (enhance scientific research and upgrade technological capabilities), target 16.10 (ensure public access to information and protect fundamental freedoms), and target 17.8 (enhance the use of enabling technology).

[Introduction to Data Privacy](https://www.ntnu.edu/studies/courses/IMT4217/2025/1#tab=omEmnet)
> Data privacy has a significant overlap with information security. Importantly, information security is a prerequisite for privacy. Many organizational techniques from information security are also applicable to maintaining data privacy. However, data privacy differs from information security in important ways. One key difference lies in controlling the inferences that can be made from given data and information. This course is centered on introducing the quantitative theory behind this inference control, often referred to as statistical disclosure control. We discuss formal definitions of privacy in databases containing population data. In particular, we contrast syntactic and differential privacy and their respective suitability for balancing privacy costs with information benefits. We spend significant time studying differentially private algorithms for querying data. In addition, we discuss ethical and political arguments for why privacy is needed, and the framing of privacy as a trade-off between individual privacy and societal benefit. In this context, we critique current popular technical and regulatory approaches to data privacy from a quantitative theory perspective.
>
> According to the UN Declaration of Human Rights, privacy is a fundamental human right. Data privacy reduces information asymmetries and therefore reduces inequalities in power between individuals, corporations, and other institutions. It reduces possibilities for persecution, allows dissent, and is intrinsically connected to democracy. As such, it touches on a wide range of the UN Sustainable Development Goals, including Gender Equality (5), Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure (9), Reduced Inequalities (10), Sustainable Cities and Communities (11), and Peace, Justice and Strong Institutions (16).

[Introduction to Artificial Intelligence](https://www.ntnu.edu/studies/courses/TDT4136/2025/1#tab=omEmnet)
> This subject starts with a description of problem-solving methods based on heuristic search. After that, it covers various knowledge representation languages and inference methods for automatic problem solving. Representations in the form of predicate logic, frames, and semantic nets are treated and connected to the main forms of reasoning, especially rule-based reasoning. It also introduces architectures that integrate different reasoning methods, agent-based architectures, and architectures for interactive problem solving. Numerous application examples are given to demonstrate these methods.
