This page presents my civic engagement and the actions I have carried out in areas that matter to me. It also serves as an official proof of engagement, required as part of my engineering diploma path.

My goal with these actions is simple: help people and make a real impact on society through concrete contributions. Today, my main commitment is through WOCSA activities. In parallel, I am also progressing through rescue-related certifications.

### About WOCSA

The [World Open CyberSecurity Association](https://wocsa.org/) (WOCSA) is a non-profit organization dedicated to improving global cybersecurity awareness, education, and solutions through open collaboration. As a member, I share this vision: cybersecurity is a public concern, and knowledge should remain accessible, practical, and community-driven.

I am an actively involved member of WOCSA, contributing to workshops focused on IoT and cybersecurity awareness. I currently contribute as:
- Ethical Hacking workshop creator and presenter
- Cyberjutsu session presenter
- Association representative at fairs and public events
- WOCSA board member in 2025 as secretary

Since joining WOCSA, I have had the privilege of leading workshops and engaging with newcomers, professionals, and cybersecurity enthusiasts. WOCSA gives me the opportunity to make a meaningful civic contribution in a non-profit organization whose values align closely with mine.

### About Rescue

Another important part of my civic engagement is first-aid and rescue training with the Protection Civile organization. I am currently building rescue skills. I already validated the first level, **PSC** (Citizen First Aid), and I am now preparing for the next levels: **PSE1** and **PSE2**.

<div style="text-align: center; margin: 1rem 0 1.25rem 0;">
  <img src="/assets/legacy/img/civic-engagement/logo_protection_civile.png" alt="Protection Civile" style="max-width: 280px; width: 100%; height: auto;" />
</div>

For me, these competencies are a way to make sure I am able to help someone in need, for example in the street or in emergency situations.

Recent events in France, including attacks such as the Bataclan tragedy in Paris, also remind us how important it is that more citizens are trained and ready to react.
